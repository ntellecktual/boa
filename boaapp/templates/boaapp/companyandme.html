{# American Airlines - Baggage System #}

Who Needs? (Stakeholders)

  • Baggage Handlers: Responsible for loading, unloading, and transferring baggage.
  • Airport Staff: Involved in various roles supporting airport operations, such as customer service and security.
  • Passengers: Directly affected by baggage delays or losses.
  • Airline Operations: Need real-time information for efficient flight operations and customer service.

Why This? (No "Baggage")

  • Purpose: To track and manage baggage movement throughout the airline's network, ensuring efficient operations and reducing losses.
  • Resolve: To address challenges such as baggage delays, lost luggage, and inefficiencies in baggage handling processes.

What Functionality? (Intricate Level)

  • Predictive analytics: Use machine learning to predict baggage trends and identify potential issues.
  • Automation: Automate routine tasks to improve efficiency and reduce errors.
  • Data integration: Integration with various systems, such as flight information and passenger personal and baggage data.

How it Works? (Technical Stack)

  • Data Engineering: Azure Data Lake, Databricks, Cloud Storage
  • Machine Learning/Automation/Visualization: Python, Power Automate, Power BI
  • Security: Coverity, Black Duck, Tanium, Nucleus

{# American Airlines - Staff Management System #}

Who Needs? (Stakeholders)

  • Gate Agents: Directly responsible for managing passenger boarding and gate operations.
  • Pilots and Flight Crew: Need to access information regarding their flight schedules and assignments.
  • Airport Staff: Involved in various roles supporting airport operations, such as customer service, maintenance, and security.

Why This? (Seamless Flights)

  • Purpose: To provide a centralized platform for managing in-airport staff activities, ensuring efficient operations and accurate tracking of flight-related information.
  • Resolve: To address challenges such as manual data entry, delays in accessing critical information, and inefficiencies in coordinating staff assignments.

What Functionality? (Intricate Level)

  • Data analytics: Analyze large datasets to identify trends, optimize operations, and improve decision-making.
  • Integration: Integrate with other airport systems, such as passenger management systems and flight operations systems.
  • Mobile accessibility: Enable staff to access the application on mobile devices for on-the-go information and updates.
  • Staff scheduling: Track and manage staff schedules, assignments, and shifts.
  • Flight information: Provide real-time updates on flight status, gate assignments, and passenger boarding information.
  • Terminal and gate management: Manage terminal flight rollouts, gate assignments, and resource allocation.

How it Works? (Technical Stack)

  • Frontend/Backend: React, Angular, or Vue.js for building the user interface. Node.js or Python (Django or Flask) for developing the application's server-side logic.
  • Database: PostgreSQL or MongoDB for storing and managing application data.
  • Cloud/DevOps: Azure for hosting the application and its infrastructure. Git for version control, Jenkins or CircleCI for continuous integration, and Docker or Kubernetes for containerization and deployment.

Explore these and other technologies in the American Airlines section of the Technical Showcase.
